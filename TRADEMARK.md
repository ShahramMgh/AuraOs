# The AuraOS name

**The code is free. The name is a promise.**

AuraOS is licensed under the [AGPL-3.0](LICENSE) — you may use, study,
modify, and redistribute the code, including as a hosted service, provided you
keep it open (that is the whole point; see [AI-MANIFEST.md](AI-MANIFEST.md) and
the [README](README.md)). Nothing here restricts what the AGPL grants you.

What this document reserves is the **name, logo, and branding** — "AuraOS"
— which the AGPL deliberately does *not* license. This is the same model
Linux, Firefox, and countless projects use: free code, protected name. It is
the mechanism that keeps forks honest without ever locking the source.

## Why

The four pillars — **Privacy · Capability · Transparency · User Sovereignty** —
are the reason this project exists. A permissive-with-the-name policy would let
anyone ship something that betrays those pillars (telemetry, hidden cloud
dependence, invisible sensor access, a self-granting AI) and still call it
"AuraOS," trading on the trust the name carries. The name exists so a
user who sees it can rely on what it stands for.

## The rule

You **may**, without asking:

- Fork, modify, and redistribute the code under the AGPL-3.0.
- State truthfully that your work is *"based on AuraOS"* or *"a fork of
  AuraOS,"* with a link to the original — indeed, [NOTICE](NOTICE)
  **requires** you to credit the original.
- Use the name in good faith to refer to this project.

You **may not**, without written permission from the project maintainer:

- Call your distribution, product, or service **"AuraOS"** (or a
  confusingly similar name), or use its logo/branding, **unless** your build is
  faithful to [AI-MANIFEST.md](AI-MANIFEST.md) and this project's four pillars —
  specifically: off/local by default, no telemetry, the OS as the final
  authority over apps and models, visible sensor access, and reversible,
  inspectable permissions/network/memory.

If your fork changes those things, that is your right under the AGPL — just give
it your own name.

## Faithful build, in practice

A build may carry the name "AuraOS" only if every item in the conformance
checklist at the end of [AI-MANIFEST.md](AI-MANIFEST.md) still answers "yes."
If any answer is "no," it is a different OS — and should wear a different name.
